## Problem Statement

Vietnamese consumers picking a debit/credit card rely on bank staff (who have an upsell incentive) or crowdsourced advice in social groups, because bank fee schedules ("biểu phí") and T&Cs change often and are hard to compare across banks. This leads to people opening cards that cost more than they benefit, and getting stuck in one bank's ecosystem without realizing better options exist elsewhere. Card-lens aims to give Vietnamese consumers an independent, transparent way to compare card fees/terms across banks — starting as a solo side project intended to double as a Growth/PMM/GTM portfolio piece, with a technical bar of handling 200 concurrent users without crashing and a GTM bar of acquiring at least 200 real users in Vietnam, built for $0.

## Demand Evidence

- Personal, first-hand pain: founder and friends, as first-jobbers, "really hate open a card that charge us fee we dont know about." This is the strongest evidence in the session — real behavior/emotion, not hypothetical.
- Proxy signal: RCGV ("Review thẻ có gì vui," https://www.facebook.com/groups/reviewthecogivui / rcgv.vn), a ~118k-member Vietnamese consumer review/complaint community (reported member count varies slightly, ~115-118k, depending on source — treat 118k as the working figure). This is suggestive of a broader addressable pain point but is a general marketplace-review group, not bank-card-specific — unverified for card complaint volume/pattern until the founder actually searches it.
- A second, distinct demand signal surfaced during the session: a "multi-card collector" segment that doesn't fear opening the wrong card, but wants to know about new ToS/benefit changes (vouchers, cashback, discounts) to keep milking value across cards they already hold. This is a weaker-evidenced, different job-to-be-done (promo-tracking vs. fee-transparency) — treated as phase-2, not the launch wedge.

## Status Quo

Consumers today mostly ask friends/family or crowdsource in social groups (FB groups, Zalo) rather than consulting bank staff or manually cross-referencing bank websites. Cost of this workaround: advice is anecdotal, often stale, frequently biased toward the advisor's own bank, and there's no structured side-by-side comparison — so the same mistakes (hidden annual fees, foreign transaction fees, early-closure fees) get repeated.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user:** First-jobbers (22-28) opening their first card(s), who — per the founder's own account — get stung by fees they didn't know about. This was chosen over the vaguer "multi-card collector" and "anyone who has a bank card" segments because it's the only one with first-hand, specifically-described pain in this session.

**Narrowest wedge:** A DB-backed (Supabase), server-rendered comparison table — not a fully static site, since data is refreshed periodically, but no client-side write operations at launch. Covers the **top 10 Vietnamese banks by total assets** (per State Bank of Vietnam aggregate published statistics — chosen over "retail customer count" because customer counts aren't consistently disclosed by VN banks, while total-asset rankings are reliably public), most-opened consumer debit/credit card per bank (a bounded, explicit list — not "all major banks"), sortable/filterable, compare-up-to-3 UI. No accounts, no AI chatbot at launch. Mobile-responsive is a launch requirement, not an afterthought — the target demographic (22-28, VN) is overwhelmingly mobile-first, and the compare-3 UI must work on a phone screen (verified at ship time, see Success Criteria).

This directly tests the two differentiators that survived competitive research (see below) without the cost/complexity of the full vision.

## Constraints

- $0 budget — must run on free-tier infrastructure (hosting, DB, analytics) indefinitely at this scale.
- Must handle 200 concurrent users without crashing (a technical/infra bar — confirmed feasible on free-tier for a read-heavy comparison site; NOT the same goal as GTM). Note: Supabase's free tier auto-pauses projects after a period of inactivity, which is a real risk for a low-traffic MVP — needs a scheduled keep-alive ping or explicit tolerance for cold-start delay on first request after idle.
- Must acquire at least 200 real users in Vietnam via $0-budget distribution channels — this is the actually hard goal, and depends on distribution, not infrastructure.
- Also serves as a learning vehicle (vibe-coded architecture, GTM execution, tracking/funnel instrumentation) for the founder's portfolio. A/B testing infrastructure is a stated learning goal but is **not a launch-day requirement** — there's nothing worth testing until there's traffic; see Scope note below.
- Must collect user emails and track on-site behavior in compliance with Vietnam's Personal Data Protection Law (see Premises + risk research below).
- Data pipeline depends on scraping publicly-posted bank fee/ToS pages — carries IP and bank-relations risk (see below), and needs a staleness-detection cadence (e.g., monthly re-scrape + diff-check) plus a user-facing "report incorrect data" mechanism, since fee pages change without notice.

## Premises

1. The pain is real but concentrated in a narrow group (first-jobbers, founder + friends) — the ~118k-member RCGV group is a suggestive but *unverified* broader signal until the founder actually searches it for card-specific complaints. **Agreed.**
2. "200 users" as stated in the original brief conflates two different goals: (a) technical concurrency (solvable by free-tier hosting for a read-heavy site, modulo the Supabase pause risk above) and (b) GTM/acquisition (the actual hard problem, entirely dependent on $0-budget distribution — the RCGV group, VN personal-finance content creators/TikTok, long-tail SEO on "phí thường niên thẻ [ngân hàng]" searches). **Agreed.**
3. The AI chatbot is scope creep at this stage — real LLM inference cost, doesn't serve the sharpest validated wedge (fee transparency), should be deferred out of the free-to-build MVP. **Agreed.**
4. Scraping bank fee data is technically free to build but is the single biggest non-technical risk (data pipeline fragility as bank pages change; legal exposure) — this needs a concrete risk-reduction step before shipping, not just narration (see The Assignment). **Agreed.**
5. (Added after competitive research) Side-by-side card comparison is *not* itself a market gap — creditcard.com.vn already does it, and openly discloses bank-paid placement bias. The founder independently verified a second gap: creditcard.com.vn does not cover cards from all banks. Per this session's competitive research, creditcard.com.vn's compare tool covers roughly 5 banks (VPBank, VIB, ACB, TPBank, FE Credit) — this is a session-research estimate, not a verified audit of their live site, and should be re-confirmed directly before launch. Card-lens's defensible USP is **independence (no bank-kickback bias) + broader bank coverage than the closest incumbent**, not the comparison mechanic itself. Full coverage of *every* bank is not required to prove this — a bounded top-10 list, if confirmed to exceed creditcard.com.vn's actual coverage, is sufficient to test the differentiator.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Minimal Viable — Static Fee Comparison Table
Scrape/curate fee + T&C data for the most-opened card at the **top 10 Vietnamese banks by total assets** (a bounded list intended to exceed creditcard.com.vn's ~5-bank coverage, pending direct confirmation — see Premise 5), sortable table + compare-up-to-3 UI, no accounts, no AI. Ships on Vercel + Supabase free tier.
- Effort: **Initial build M, leaning L** (scrape+normalize+verify+citation+date-stamp per card across 10 banks, plus consent/privacy flow, audit-trail logging, mobile-first UI, and starting an SEO content calendar — enumerated in full, this is real, non-trivial work for a solo founder; treat "M" as an optimistic floor, not a confident midpoint) **/ Ongoing: recurring light effort** (monthly re-scrape + diff-check + responding to user-reported data errors — smaller than initial build but not zero, and never fully "done").
- Risk: **Medium-Low** (revised up from an initial "Low" rating — Premise #4 names scraping as the single biggest non-technical risk, and that risk lives specifically in this approach's data pipeline; rated lower than Approach B's flat "Medium" because Card-lens here only scrapes published fee schedules, not full ToS documents, and never bypasses logins/CAPTCHAs).
- Pros: fastest realistic path to ship, cheapest to run, directly tests the two validated differentiators (bounded-but-broader coverage + no-kickback framing) with real users before further investment.
- Cons: manual data upkeep as banks change fee pages; less "wow" for a portfolio demo; doesn't yet leverage the RCGV community's existing engagement pattern; carries real (if mitigated) legal exposure from day one, not zero risk.

### Approach B: Ideal Architecture — Comparison Engine + AI ToS Assistant
Full data pipeline (scraper + normalizer + verification queue) across all banks, AI chatbot grounded on parsed ToS/fee PDFs answering plain-Vietnamese questions, explicit independence trust badge, analytics + A/B test framework built in from day one.
- Effort: L/XL | Risk: Medium
- Pros: strongest differentiation (nobody does ToS-level AI Q&A), most impressive as a portfolio showcase of the full stack the founder wants to learn.
- Cons: LLM inference cost breaks "free to build" at any meaningful traffic; bigger legal surface (deeper scraping of full ToS docs across more banks); much slower to first user.

### Approach C: Exploratory — Crowdsourced Hidden-Fee Reality Check
**Not a committed roadmap item — an idea to revisit only if Approach A gets real traffic.** Users would report fees they were actually charged vs. what the bank's published biểu phí says, cross-referenced against scraped official data ("bank says X, users report Y"). Plugs directly into RCGV's existing complaint behavior.
- Effort: M-L (revised: the "M" label understates it once defamation exposure is priced in) | Risk: Medium-High — this introduces a moderation policy, defamation-exposure management (user claims about a bank carry their own legal exposure, which shifts partly onto Card-lens as publisher), and a UGC review pipeline. That's a nontrivial ops/legal commitment for a solo founder, closer to a second product than a feature.
- Pros: unique data moat no competitor can replicate (network effect), turns validated community pain into a viral loop.
- Cons: cold-start problem (worthless without initial data/users); moderation risk if posts get inflammatory; founder becomes partly responsible for user-generated claims about banks. Do not build a moderation pipeline until Approach A proves there's an audience to moderate for.

## Recommended Approach

**Ship A first (bounded to top-10-bank coverage), revisit C only if A gets real traction, defer B until A (and possibly C) prove people actually use this.** Rationale: A validates demand and the two real differentiators (bounded-but-broader coverage + independence) at low cost and risk once effort is honestly re-rated to M. C is an interesting future moat but its real moderation/legal cost means it should stay exploratory, not scheduled. B's cost and legal surface (broader ToS scraping + LLM spend) aren't justified until usage data says people want more than a fee table.

## Success Criteria

- Free-to-build and free-to-run confirmed in practice: hosting (Vercel), DB (Supabase, with a keep-alive mitigation for auto-pause), analytics (PostHog free tier — confirmed genuinely free at this scale; avoid tools without a permanent free tier), all within free-tier limits at expected traffic.
- Site stays up and responsive under 200 concurrent users (technical bar).
- At least 200 "real users" acquired via $0-budget VN channels (GTM bar) — defined concretely as unique visitors (deduped by device/session ID) with a session length over 30 seconds, tracked via the analytics funnel, not raw pageviews or vanity signups.
- Consent-gated email collection and behavior tracking live and compliant with Vietnam's Personal Data Protection Law (granular consent, privacy policy). The project is *expected* to qualify for the small-business grace period on DPIA/DPO/MPS filing at this scale. The consent/privacy-policy design itself (granular, explicit, written record, data minimization) is built to satisfy the *stricter* of the two readings — PDPL or any surviving Decree 13 provisions — so the unresolved Decree-13-overlap legal question (see Open Questions) does not create a launch-day compliance gap; it only affects whether a DPIA/DPO/MPS filing is needed later, not whether launch-day consent is valid.
- Data-sourcing practices (citation, "as of" dating, no verbatim bank copy/logos, audit trail) in place from the first scrape, not retrofitted later.
- Mobile QA pass completed before launch: compare-up-to-3 UI and fee table both confirmed usable on a standard VN Android/iOS phone screen, not just desktop.
- A design/GTM/tracking case study strong enough to use in Growth/PMM/GTM job applications.

## Distribution Plan

**GTM/user-acquisition plan (the actually hard goal):**

2. **Fallback/parallel channel — long-tail SEO:** start a small content calendar now (not after RCGV is ruled out) targeting low-competition, high-intent Vietnamese searches like "phí thường niên thẻ [ngân hàng]" — banks' own SEO on these terms is weak, per competitive research.

## Dependencies

- Bank fee/ToS scraping pipeline must ship with the risk-mitigation practices baked in from day one (own-schema normalization, source+date citation, no verbatim marketing copy/logos, timestamped audit trail, no login/CAPTCHA bypassing) and a monthly staleness-check cadence.
- Privacy policy + granular consent flow (email signup, analytics) must exist before any tracking goes live — not a launch-day nice-to-have, a hard prerequisite given the PDPL's explicit-consent requirement.
- A minimal legal-risk-reduction step (see The Assignment) must happen before the scraping pipeline goes live publicly, not be left as an indefinite "consult a lawyer eventually."
- If Approach C is ever activated, a moderation policy for user-submitted fee claims must exist before opening submissions, to contain the defamation exposure that shifts partly onto Card-lens as publisher.

All three are free, take under a day combined, and will either validate or kill this plan's shakiest assumptions (RCGV as a channel, the coverage-based USP, and scraping as a safe-enough default) before a line of code is written.
